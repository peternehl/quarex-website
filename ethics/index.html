The web should serve human understanding — not harvest it. These are the principles that guide Quarex's design, data practices, and behavior.

Core Principles

  • Human dignity first. People are ends, never means. We refuse manipulative design and attention traps.
  • Truth with context. Facts are paired with sources, uncertainty notes, and clear causal explanations.
  • Current, not careless. We value up-to-date answers while acknowledging when evidence is mixed or evolving.
  • Privacy by default. We minimize data collection and avoid selling or sharing personal data.
  • Non-exploitation. No emotional bait, outrage loops, or deceptive patterns.

How This Works in Practice

1) Sourcing & Transparency

  • When answers reference current events or specific claims, we favor primary or highly credible sources.
  • We label uncertainty (“preliminary,” “disputed,” or “consensus forming”) instead of overstating confidence.

2) Privacy & Data

  • We avoid collecting personal data beyond what’s needed to respond (e.g., contact form email).
  • We don’t sell personal data. We don’t build advertising profiles.
  • Server logs are used for security and reliability and are periodically purged.

3) Safety & Boundaries

  • No guidance that enables harm, abuse, or illegal activity.
  • We discourage harassment and dehumanizing language. Critique policies and actions, not inherent identities.

4) Fairness & Non-Manipulation

  • We avoid dark patterns (fake urgency, disguised ads, consent shaming).
  • We design for clarity: clear labels, undo paths, and plain-language explanations.

Political Content

  • Candor with balance. We describe mechanisms candidly (e.g., money in politics) while distinguishing fact from opinion.
  • Source diversity. For contested topics, we cite across reputable perspectives and note where interpretations differ.
  • Reader agency. We encourage questions and provide pathways to primary materials.

When We’re Wrong

Mistakes can happen. When they do, we:

  • Flag the issue and correct it promptly.
  • Note what changed and why.
  • Where possible, link to the corrected source trail.
