Subject: A 30-Minute Civics Lesson That Won't Get You Called Into the Office

Hi [First Name],

Here's a lesson plan that works right now:

1. Students enter their zip code and identify the races on their ballot (5 min)
2. Each student picks a candidate and reads the structured profile (10 min)
3. Students identify one claim, trace it to its source, and evaluate the source (10 min)
4. Class discussion: What surprised you? What couldn't you find? (5 min)

No prep required on your end beyond opening the link. The content is structured, the sources are visible, and there is nothing in the system that takes a partisan position.

If a parent asks where the information came from, every answer has a citation. If an administrator asks about bias, the platform has no editorial voice, no opinion section, and no ads.
