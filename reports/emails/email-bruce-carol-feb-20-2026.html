Switched from Gemini to Claude

The "Ask Quarex" feature now runs on Claude Sonnet 4.5 instead of Google's Gemini. Here's why:

  • Better reasoning — Claude handles nuanced political and historical questions more carefully. It doesn't oversimplify or dodge complexity.
  • More consistent tone — Gemini sometimes felt like it was hedging or being overly cautious. Claude gives direct answers while still being balanced.
  • Live web search — Claude can now search the web in real-time, so when you ask about current events, you get today's news, not six-month-old training data.

Try it: Ask Quarex

The response quality difference is noticeable. Ask it something about the 2026 elections or current policy debates and you'll see what I mean.

Public Studies Curricula

Public Studies now has 21 structured curricula across 8 schools. Each curriculum pulls from Quarex books and organizes them into a learning path.

Some highlights:

  • Hispanic Studies — 6 curricula covering culture, politics, identity, and history (available in English and Spanish)
  • Women's Studies — Feminism, reproductive rights, workplace equality, global perspectives
  • Black Studies — From the Atlantic slave trade through contemporary activism
  • Media Studies — Propaganda, misinformation, media literacy
  • AI Studies — Foundations, ethics, real-world applications
  • Labor Studies — Union history, gig economy, workplace rights

The full list: publicstudies.org

Each curriculum is free, no login required. Just structured learning paths through the material.
