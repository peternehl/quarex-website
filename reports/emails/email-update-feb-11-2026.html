Quarex Update: Marketing Partnership, New Book, and Platform Polish

Three updates this week — a new partnership, a new book on how immigration became a political weapon, and some visual refinements that make the platform feel more cohesive.

Marketing Partnership with Momentum AI

Quarex has partnered with Momentum AI (Paige Rucker Orme) for a structured marketing engagement. Paige brings experience in AI-augmented content strategy and audience development — exactly what a platform this ambitious needs as it scales.

The engagement kicked off this week with a focus on building a marketing portal, refining the platform's positioning, and developing outreach strategies.

Paige's focus is on positioning Quarex for broader audiences — making the platform's value proposition clear to people who've never heard of a "Living Book" or a "Curiosity Engine." The goal is traffic, engagement, and eventually sustainability.

New Book: How Trump Subverted and Weaponized Immigration Reform

This week's major content addition is a 15-chapter book that traces how bipartisan immigration reform died and how the issue became a permanent political weapon.

The book covers:

  • The bipartisan history from Reagan's 1986 amnesty through the Gang of Eight
  • The 2016 campaign and the "build the wall" phenomenon
  • Family separation, DACA hostage politics, and the asylum crackdown
  • How Trump torpedoed the 2024 bipartisan border bill
  • The second term: mass deportation, ICE as political theater, and the Newark church raid
  • What functional immigration reform could have accomplished

The book includes a chapter specifically addressing how weaponizing immigration mirrors the Republican playbook on abortion — keeping the issue alive for political benefit rather than solving it.

This is in the Immigration Debates shelf under Contested Issues, alongside related books on DACA, the asylum system, and the immigration wedge.

Platform Styling Updates

Some visual polish this week to make the platform more cohesive across all pages:

  • Logo visibility: The Quarex logo now appears consistently across the library navigation, reader, and main site. A subtle blue glow effect makes it pop against the dark theme without being distracting.
  • Dark theme consistency: The color palette (#0A0A0A background, #7CB9FF accent) is now uniform across all pages — libraries, search, reports, and the home page.
  • Path fixes: Switched from relative to absolute paths for assets, eliminating broken image issues when navigating between sections.

These aren't flashy changes, but they matter. A platform that looks polished feels more trustworthy. As Quarex grows, first impressions count.
