What’s No Longer Needed

The original proposal centered on building knowableonline.org, including audience portals for Voters, Educators, Civic Leaders, and Investors. That site now exists with a different architecture — organized around Schools and Curricula rather than audience personas. The core deliverable of all three original options has been completed independently.

There is no site that needs to be built. What needs to happen is adoption.

The Problem Now

The Quarex ecosystem contains 647 books, 4 complete curricula, and full 2026 election coverage across 5 interconnected sites. Almost no one knows it exists.

The system works. The content is real. But content without distribution is inventory, not impact. The challenge is no longer building — it’s getting the right people and institutions to discover, trust, and use what’s been built.

What We Need

Three things

1. Messaging That Explains Quarex to Non-Technical Audiences

Quarex is easy to explain to technologists. It is harder to explain to a school librarian, a civics teacher, a county elections officer, or a nonprofit director. We need clear, tested language that communicates the value in under 30 seconds for each audience. Not marketing copy — clarity.

2. Deployable Materials for Institutional Partners

A one-pager for educators. A one-pager for civic organizations. A short pitch for libraries. QR materials that link directly to curricula and election tools. Social media graphics that can be posted without further editing. These materials need to be finished — ready to hand to someone or drop in an email.

3. Targeted Outreach to Real Institutions

Not mass marketing. Specific outreach to the kinds of organizations most likely to adopt a free, nonpartisan, structured knowledge platform: public libraries, community colleges, voter education nonprofits, civics teachers, journalism programs, and media literacy organizations. The goal is 50 institutional contacts with tailored messaging.

Design Unification (Optional Add-On)

The five Quarex sites were built at different times with slightly different design languages. They share a dark theme and the same fonts (Playfair Display + Inter), but accent colors, component styles, and navigation patterns vary. A professional design pass to create a shared design system — a common header/footer pattern, consistent card styles, unified navigation — would make the ecosystem feel like one platform rather than five related sites.

This is not essential for adoption, but it would strengthen credibility with institutional partners who evaluate platforms partly on visual coherence.

Content Corrections (Still Required)

Non-negotiable: Any materials produced for outreach must use accurate data only. The corrections from the original proposal remain in effect:

  • No fabricated testimonials. Use aspirational framing or real deployment scenarios, never fake quotes.
  • Accurate statistics only. The real numbers are strong: 647 books, 3,369 chapters, 4 curricula, all 50 states covered for 2026 elections. Use them.
  • No features that don’t exist. Describe what Quarex actually does. The reality is already more than any competitor offers.

Why This Engagement

Building the Quarex ecosystem is something one person with AI can do. Getting institutions to trust and adopt it is not. Trust is built through relationships, clear messaging, and showing up consistently in the places where educators, librarians, and civic leaders already look. That requires a different kind of work.

The system exists. The content is real. The architecture is sound. What’s missing is the bridge between what’s been built and the people who would use it if they knew it was there.
