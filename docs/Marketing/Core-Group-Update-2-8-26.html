Quarex Updates: Adaptive Responses, Hispanic Studies, Multilingual Elections

February 8, 2026

Team,

Three updates to share:

1. Echeloned Response Levels

The Ask interface now includes Introductory, Intermediate, and Advanced selectors. Users choose the complexity level that matches their background, and answers are tailored accordingly. This addresses one of the core challenges in educational AI: meeting learners where they are rather than assuming a single audience.

2. Hispanic Studies Program — Four Complete Curricula

We’ve launched a comprehensive Hispanic Studies program with 79 courses and nearly 950 chapters:

  • Hispanic Cultures & Identity in America — Who is “Hispanic”? The distinct communities, regional patterns, foodways, music, religion, and evolving identity
  • U.S.-Latin America Relations — From the Monroe Doctrine through Cold War coups to NAFTA and the border, plus the deep roots of Hispanic communities predating the U.S.
  • The Hispanic American Experience Today — Immigration, the undocumented, DACA, labor, education, healthcare, political power, criminal justice, discrimination
  • Hispanic Contributions to American Culture — Literature, visual arts, Hollywood, theater, music, dance, cuisine, architecture, science, medicine, space, sports, business, military service, journalism, education

All content originates in English, but the AI can deliver answers and generate follow-up questions in Spanish — making the material accessible to Spanish-dominant learners and heritage speakers.

Syllabi are live at publicstudies.org.

3. Election 2026 — 13 Languages

election2026.net now provides candidate information in 13 languages, ensuring that voters across linguistic communities can access the same information about who’s running and what they stand for.
