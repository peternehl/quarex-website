Paul,

I wanted to share a tool I've been building called Quarex that I think you'll find useful, especially given your interest in civic engagement and informed decision-making.

The Problem

George Santos proved that a congressional candidate can fabricate an entire biography - fake jobs, fake degrees, fake charity work - and still get elected. The information to catch him existed, but voters in NY-03 didn't know what questions to ask.

This isn't unique to Santos. Voters face 435 House races, 33 Senate races, and 39 Governor races in 2026. Nobody has time to develop a research framework for each one.

What Quarex Does

Quarex provides pre-loaded accountability questions for every candidate in every federal race. When you select a candidate, you get 11 research questions with their name automatically inserted:

  • Does {name} have a criminal record, history of bankruptcy, or any other significant legal issues?
  • What false or misleading public claims has {name} made in the last 30 days?
  • What conflicts of interest exist between {name}'s public role and their financial, business, or personal ties?
  • Who funds {name}'s campaign, and in what approximate amounts?
  • What is {name}'s long-term record of truthfulness, accuracy, and public credibility?

You copy any question and paste it into an AI assistant (Claude, ChatGPT, Perplexity). The AI does the research. You get the answers.

Example: Select "John Smith (R)" from Ohio's 4th District, and the question becomes: "Does John Smith have a criminal record, history of bankruptcy, or any other significant legal issues?"

Current Coverage

  • 2,003 candidates across 382 House districts
  • 319 candidates across 33 Senate races
  • 397 candidates across 39 Governor races

Updated weekly as new candidates declare.

Why This Matters

The same questions apply to every candidate - Democratic, Republican, Independent. It's not about catching one party; it's about giving voters a consistent framework for due diligence that they otherwise wouldn't have.

Santos-style fraud succeeds when voters don't know what to ask. Quarex makes those questions available to everyone, for free, for every race.
